# User Persona: The Performance Marketing Manager

## At a glance
- **Name:** Jamie Chen (composite persona)
- **Role:** Performance Marketing Manager / Senior Paid Media Manager
- **Industry:** Consumer goods, retail, direct-to-consumer brands
- **Company size:** Mid-market to enterprise ($10M–$500M annual revenue)
- **Team size:** 2–6 in-house; often supplemented by an agency or bid management tool (Pacvue, Skai, Perpetua)
- **Geography:** North America primarily; growing presence in EU, JP, AU

## Goals
- Drive measurable sales volume and ROAS from Amazon ad spend — with clear visibility into which campaigns are working and where to shift budget
- Expand beyond Sponsored Products into DSP and Streaming TV to build a defensible full-funnel strategy
- Prove the value of Amazon Ads investment to leadership (CMO, VP of E-commerce) with credible, attribution-backed data
- Reduce ACoS while maintaining or growing revenue against flat or marginally growing budgets

## Pain points
- Rising CPCs as more brands compete for the same high-intent keywords, eroding ROAS on Sponsored Products
- DSP and Streaming TV are hard to justify internally — last-touch attribution undervalues upper-funnel spend and the numbers "don't look as good" as Sponsored Products
- Historically, managing Sponsored Ads and DSP required separate consoles, separate logins, and manual report stitching (partially resolved by Campaign Manager in Nov 2025)
- Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC) is the right tool for cross-campaign measurement, but SQL requirements and technical setup made it inaccessible without an agency or data partner

## How they discover and buy
Amazon Ads is primarily self-serve and product-led — Jamie signed up through Seller Central or the Amazon Ads console. As DSP spend grew, her brand engaged Amazon's enterprise sales team. Agency partners handle some managed-service DSP activations. Budget authority sits with the Head of Performance Marketing or VP of E-commerce; Jamie recommends strategy and owns execution.

## A day in the life
Jamie starts her morning reviewing yesterday's Sponsored Products metrics in the Amazon Ads console — scanning ACoS and conversion rate by keyword, pausing two underperformers, and adjusting bids on high-converting ad groups. Mid-morning, she pulls a DSP report and spends twenty minutes manually reconciling it against Sponsored Products data in a Google Sheet — a workflow she's been meaning to fix for months. After lunch, she joins a call with their agency partner to walk through an AMC path-to-conversion analysis the agency ran on her behalf, since her team doesn't have anyone fluent in SQL. Before end of day, she prepares a performance slide for the quarterly business review, translating ROAS and new-to-brand numbers into language her CMO will find compelling.
