# User Persona: The Spectrum Residential Customer

## At a glance
- **Name:** Marcus Webb (composite persona)
- **Role:** Residential broadband subscriber
- **Demographics:** 34, married, two children, homeowner
- **Location:** Suburban midwest — Charter's core coverage geography
- **Tenure:** 6-year Spectrum customer; internet + TV bundle, recently added Spectrum Mobile for two lines
- **Primary contact channel:** Calls IVR first; switches to chat if wait time exceeds 5 minutes
- **Tech comfort:** Moderate — uses the My Spectrum app occasionally, prefers to talk to someone for billing issues

## Goals
- Resolve service issues quickly without repeating himself across channels or agents
- Understand his bill without having to call — pricing changes after promotional periods cause recurring confusion
- Trust that the AI assistant he reaches actually knows his account before the conversation starts
- Get credit or resolution on service disruptions without escalating to a human agent

## Pain points
- **Context loss at channel boundaries:** Called IVR, got transferred to chat, had to re-explain the same issue from scratch. Happens frequently enough that he prefers human agents despite longer wait times.
- **Reactive-only service:** Found out about an outage in his area only after calling to report it — the system didn't proactively notify him or offer credit.
- **Bill confusion on recurring contacts:** Called three times in 90 days about the same promotional rate expiration. Each time he was "resolved" but the underlying confusion wasn't addressed.
- **IVR that deflects instead of resolves:** Has learned to say "agent" repeatedly because the bot routes him to information he could find on the website — not to action on his account.

## How Marcus contacts Spectrum
Inbound only — Marcus doesn't initiate contact unless something is wrong. Triggers are: bill higher than expected, service outage or degradation, equipment issue, or a rate change he didn't anticipate. He calls first (~60% of contacts), uses chat if call queue is long, and uses the app only for payment. He has never initiated a proactive chat.

## A day in the life
Marcus notices his internet has been dropping intermittently for two days — affecting his work-from-home calls. He opens the My Spectrum app, sees no outage notification, and calls the IVR. The system asks him to verify his account, runs a line test, and tells him everything looks fine — the same response he got six months ago before a technician visit confirmed a node issue. Frustrated, he asks for an agent. He's transferred to chat, where he has to re-enter his account number and re-explain the dropping connection. The agent schedules a technician for four days out. Marcus hangs up dissatisfied — not because the technician wasn't dispatched, but because the system gave him no signal it understood his history or urgency. He'll remember this interaction the next time he sees a Spectrum Mobile ad from a competitor.
