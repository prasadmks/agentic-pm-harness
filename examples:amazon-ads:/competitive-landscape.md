# Competitive Landscape: Amazon Ads

## Overview
Amazon Ads competes in digital advertising where Google and Meta are the dominant incumbents, collectively commanding the majority of global digital ad spend. Amazon's differentiation is its unique combination of first-party purchase-intent data, retail media inventory, closed-loop sales attribution, and a growing streaming ecosystem. While Google wins on search intent and Meta wins on social targeting, Amazon occupies a structurally distinct position: it connects advertising directly to measurable purchase outcomes — a capability neither competitor can replicate with equal fidelity. Retail media as a category is projected to surpass social media ad spend globally by 2028, making Amazon's structural position increasingly defensible.

## Key competitors

**Google Ads (Alphabet)** — The dominant digital advertising platform globally, crossing $200B in revenue in 2025. Strengths include unmatched search intent signals, YouTube's premium video reach, and the broadest display network (GDN). Google's weakness vs. Amazon is the absence of a direct purchase signal tied to actual transaction data — conversion tracking is indirect and dependent on advertiser-installed pixels. DV360 competes with Amazon DSP for programmatic budgets.

**Meta Ads (Facebook / Instagram)** — The leader in social advertising, commanding ~23% of global digital ad spend. Strengths include deep social graph targeting, creative-first formats (Reels, Stories), and strong direct-response performance for DTC brands. Meta's weakness vs. Amazon is the absence of first-party commerce data, ongoing signal degradation from iOS privacy changes, and limited lower-funnel attribution fidelity.

**Walmart Connect** — The #2 retail media network in the US (~8% share), growing its off-site programmatic capabilities. Directly targets the same endemic (on-marketplace) advertisers as Amazon. Walmart's advantage is physical store scale (4,600+ US locations) and Walmart+ membership data; its disadvantage is narrower digital inventory, lower e-commerce GMV vs. Amazon, and a less mature DSP and analytics stack.

## Positioning summary

**Where Amazon wins:**
- Any advertiser whose goal is measurable, purchase-linked outcomes — particularly brands selling on Amazon or wanting to reach high-intent shoppers at the moment of consideration
- Retail media: Amazon holds ~39.8% of all US retail media spend; the moat compounds as first-party data grows with Prime membership and purchase history
- Full-funnel in a single platform: awareness on Prime Video → consideration via Sponsored Brands → conversion via Sponsored Products, with consistent audience data throughout

**Where Amazon cedes ground:**
- Broad search demand capture outside the Amazon ecosystem (Google dominates)
- Social-first, creative-led awareness campaigns targeting younger demographics (Meta and TikTok)
- Non-Amazon open-web programmatic where advertisers prefer a neutral, non-conflicted DSP

**Fastest-growing moats:**
1. **Streaming TV inventory** — Prime Video's ad-supported tier gives Amazon a premium video surface with purchase attribution that no other CTV player can match
2. **Amazon Marketing Cloud (AMC)** — As privacy regulations eliminate third-party cookies, clean-room analytics become the industry standard; AMC is positioned to be the preferred privacy-safe measurement environment for brands with Amazon audiences
3. **Retail media category leadership** — The fragmented 200+ retail media networks create a widening gap vs. Amazon's scale, data quality, and self-serve tooling as the category grows toward 25% of global digital ad spend by 2028
