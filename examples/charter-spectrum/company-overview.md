# Company Overview: Charter Communications (Spectrum)

## Company snapshot
- **Company name:** Charter Communications, Inc.
- **Brand:** Spectrum
- **Website:** https://www.spectrum.com / https://enterprise.spectrum.com
- **Ticker:** NASDAQ: CHTR
- **Headquarters:** Stamford, CT
- **Stage / size:** Fortune 100 public company — ~$55B annual revenue (2024), ~101,000 employees
- **Customers:** 32M+ residential and business customers across 41 states

## Mission
Connect people and businesses to what matters most — through reliable internet, TV, mobile, and voice services — while continuously raising the bar on customer experience through AI-powered digital service.

## Business model
- **Residential broadband (primary):** Subscription-based internet, TV, and voice bundles. Internet is the anchor product; TV and voice are declining but still material.
- **Spectrum Mobile:** MVNO operating on Verizon's network. Fastest-growing segment — 9M+ lines as of 2024.
- **Spectrum Business / Enterprise:** SMB and enterprise connectivity, managed networking, and unified communications. Separate sales motion from residential.
- **Advertising Sales:** Local and regional TV advertising through Spectrum Reach. Programmatic and addressable capabilities growing.

## Founding story
Charter was founded in 1993 as a regional cable operator. Through a series of acquisitions — most significantly Time Warner Cable and Bright House Networks in 2016 — Charter became the second-largest cable operator in the US. The Spectrum brand was unified across all customer-facing products post-merger. Since 2020, Charter has invested heavily in AI-powered digital customer experience to reduce contact center costs and improve self-service resolution at scale.

## Key facts
- **32M+** residential and business customer relationships across 41 states
- **250M+** annual customer contacts across voice IVR, chat, and digital channels
- **$55B** annual revenue (2024) — internet and mobile are growth drivers
- **9M+** Spectrum Mobile lines — added ~2M lines in 2024 alone
- **80%+** self-service resolution rate on AI-powered contacts, up from 52% pre-agentic platform
- Digital service channels (IVR, chat, app) handle the majority of inbound customer contacts
- Cox merger announced 2024 — pending regulatory approval; would create the largest US cable operator
